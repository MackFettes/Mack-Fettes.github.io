Heat exchanger FYP

4th Year Individual Project · B50PR · Heriot-Watt University

Project summary

This project experimentally investigated how coil pitch, flow configuration, water flowrate, air velocity and pulsed airflow affect the overall heat transfer coefficient in a helically coiled shell-and-coil heat exchanger. Three coil pitches (19 mm, 24.5 mm and 30 mm) were tested under parallel and counter-current flow conditions.

Key findings

  • Counter-current flow outperformed parallel flow by an average of 14.7% under steady conditions
  • Air velocity was the dominant variable — increasing from 10 to 18.5 m/s raised the heat transfer coefficient by ~60%
  • Highest measured overall heat transfer coefficient: 0.245 kW/m²K at 24.5 mm pitch
  • No single coil pitch was universally optimal — each behaved as a distinct heat exchanger depending on operating conditions
  • Pulsed airflow at Strouhal number 0.2 generally reduced performance
  • Theoretical optimisation identified 19 mm pitch as highest predicted U at 0.305 kW/m²K, diverging from experimental peak — highlighting limitations in airside correlations

What this project involved

A full individual research project — I designed the experimental methodology, built and ran the test rig, collected and processed the data, applied Nusselt number correlations for theoretical comparison, and wrote a 7,700-word journal paper. It sits at the intersection of thermodynamics, fluid mechanics and experimental engineering.

Supersonic jet engine design

Group Project · B51EL Compressible Fluids · Heriot-Watt University

Project summary

With renewed commercial interest in supersonic passenger flight, this project tasked our group with designing an engine for a supersonic transport aircraft capable of operating across a range of flight conditions using both SCRAM and RAM engine configurations.

What we designed

  • Engine inlet — designed for efficient air capture and compression across supersonic flight conditions
  • Diffuser — theoretical compression staging and stagnation condition calculations
  • Combustion chamber — designed to sustain combustion at supersonic flow conditions
  • Converging-diverging nozzle — designed to expand exhaust efficiently for thrust
  • Experimental validation using a wind tunnel test rig measuring Mach number, pressure and mass flow rate
  • Pathway analysis for adapting the design to sustainable aviation fuels

What this project involved

A complex group project drawing on compressible flow theory, thermodynamics and propulsion engineering. Experimental rig issues limited full validation — which taught us as much about real engineering problem-solving as the design work itself.

NHS anti-ligature paper towel dispenser

Group Project · B50DM Industrial Design & Manufacture · Heriot-Watt University

Project summary

NHS Scotland and InnoScot Health identified a gap in the market for a truly anti-ligature paper towel dispenser suitable for medium-secure mental health environments. No existing product fully met the required safety standards. Our group of five was commissioned to design one from scratch.

What we delivered

  • Wall-mounted dispenser: PETG shell, aluminium frame, SBR seal, internal towel basket with tamper-resistant cam locks
  • Concept evaluation using AHP-weighted pairwise comparison across 5 initial concepts against 16 design requirements
  • FEA stress analysis in Abaqus — confirmed elastic response under a 1,000 N misuse load with adequate factor of safety
  • Full manufacturing process design, DFMA analysis and cost analysis — estimated unit cost £311.68 (50–60% reduction vs comparable market products)
  • Complete PFMEA, quality management plan and risk register
  • 12 of 16 design requirements fully satisfied

What this project involved

The most complete end-to-end design project I've worked on — a real client brief, real constraints, and a deliverable that could genuinely go into clinical deployment. Balancing safety, manufacturability, cost and clinical usability simultaneously, for a real NHS client, sharpened the work in a way that academic briefs alone don't.
